Shared planning controls

  • Target buffer below the cap: $5,000, giving an illustrative TSB target near $2.095 million.
  • Home baseline: $500,000, adjustable in the Command Centre.
  • Pool C external investment drag: 0.35% real per year.
  • Retirement scenarios are expressed in real dollars and use real returns unless explicitly labelled otherwise. Pre-retirement sensitivities are separately labelled nominal.

Calculation layer

Deterministic projection and annual cash ledger

Closed-form monthly projection

For a starting balance B, annual real return r, annual investment draw D, and n = years × 12 months:

monthlyReturn = (1 + r)^(1/12) - 1
monthlyDraw   = max(0, D) / 12
growth        = (1 + monthlyReturn)^n
endingBalance = max(0, B × growth - monthlyDraw × ((growth - 1) / monthlyReturn))

If monthlyReturn is zero:
endingBalance = max(0, B - monthlyDraw × n)

The Command Centre sets D = max(0, annual spending - net PSS pension). The spending frontier evaluates annual spending from $80,000 to $140,000 in $5,000 steps, projects to age 75 using an illustrative 5% real return, then adds the selected home value to show a gross estate.

Operational annual ledger

mandatoryDraw = openingPoolA × ageBasedMinimumRate
lifestyleGap  = max(0, spending - netPssPension)
actualDraw    = min(openingPoolA, max(mandatoryDraw, lifestyleGap))
reinvestment = max(0, netPssPension + actualDraw - spending)
poolCDrag     = openingPoolC × 0.0035
closingPoolA  = max(0, openingPoolA × (1 + realReturn) - actualDraw)
closingPoolC  = max(0, openingPoolC × (1 + realReturn) - poolCDrag + reinvestment)

Minimum draw rates represented in the integrated ledger are: under 65, 4%; 65–74, 5%; 75–79, 6%; 80–84, 7%; 85–89, 9%; 90–94, 11%; 95+, 14%. These are model assumptions tied to the baseline and must be reconfirmed against current law.

V23 deterministic governor

  1. Apply the age-based mandatory draw from Pool A.
  2. Use pension plus the required draw to meet the selected spending rule.
  3. Draw extra from Pool A only when the spending gap exceeds the mandatory draw.
  4. Route surplus to available super headroom when permitted, otherwise to Pool C.
  5. Keep Pool B as a transit account for wash/recontribution modelling rather than a permanent growth pool.
The static reference describes the algorithm. The interactive V23 page remains the authoritative surface for changing profile-specific floor, target, strong-estate and spending-governor settings.

Uncertainty layer

Monte Carlo, sequence and shock tests

Integrated Command Centre fan

  • 600 reproducible seeded paths from age 60 through age 95.
  • Annual returns are sampled from a normal distribution with the selected real mean and 12% volatility, clamped between −55% and +45%.
  • Each year: capital = max(0, capital × (1 + sampledReturn) - annualDraw).
  • The chart reports the P10, P25, P50, P75 and P90 balance paths. Percentiles are scenario outputs, not guarantees.

V23 stochastic lab

V23 exposes run count, expected real return, volatility, target age and guardrail thresholds. It reports estimated probabilities of clearing the selected floor, target and strong-estate levels. Sampling noise remains; V23 notes that a 500-run result may vary by roughly one to three percentage points between samples.

Sequence-risk isolation

The sequence test reorders the same set of annual returns into worst-first, smooth and best-first paths. Because the arithmetic average is held constant, the resulting balance spread isolates the effect of return order while withdrawals occur.

Stress controls

The one-off shock path applies the selected shock return at the chosen age and the mean return in other years. V23 also includes a CPI-lag stress: the real PSS pension can dip for a year during an inflation shock while capital experiences the selected real-return drag, requiring the investment draw to top up the pension shortfall.

Estate and super sequencing

NCC wash and death-benefit-tax methods

The planning objective is to reduce the taxable component exposed to an illustrative 17% adult non-tax-dependant death-benefit-tax rate, while respecting contribution eligibility, TSB/TBC headroom and transaction sequencing. The supplied 60/40 estimate uses a 29.03% tax-free and 70.97% taxable-taxed split.

Modelled sequence

  1. Before the relevant 30 June snapshot, confirm TSB, contribution eligibility, cap space and implementation advice.
  2. Commute the selected amount from Pool A under the chosen component method.
  3. Contribute the eligible NCC amount to Pool B.
  4. Recommence or consolidate according to provider and advice requirements.
  5. Preserve the intended $5,000 planning buffer and route mandatory-draw surplus according to the governor.
Implementation control: the integrated Command Centre contains a simplified 0–7 wash-cycle illustration. V23 carries the method comparison and expanded corrected analysis. Neither is an instruction to transact.

Comparators

Tax, salary equivalence, spending and estate

Salary-equivalent comparator

The dashboard compares retirement cash flow with employment salary by calculating resident individual income tax plus a 2% Medicare levy, then solving by binary search for the gross salary that produces the selected net income. The baseline tax function uses a $18,200 tax-free threshold, bracket points at $45,000, $135,000 and $190,000, rates of 30%, 37% and 45% above those points, and a lower bracket rate of 15% for 2026–27 or 14% for 2027–28.

This deliberately simplified comparator excludes offsets, Medicare levy variations, Medicare levy surcharge, HELP, deductions and household-specific effects. It is a modelled comparison, not a tax return estimate.

Spending guardrails

V23 supports three distinguishable spending modes: age-band spending; a percentage of positive investment earnings, which produces no earnings-based gap after a negative return and can require extra Pool A draw in strong years; and a percentage of the mandatory draw, which cannot exceed the mandatory draw and routes the unused surplus through the reinvestment governor.

Estate view

The simplified frontier reports gross estate = projected investments + selected home value. V23 separately tracks account components, external assets and illustrative death-benefit tax so gross and after-tax outcomes are not conflated.

Audit controls

Known mismatches and model governance

Do not mix Rail A and Rail B silently.
Each output inherits a named rail. Cross-rail comparison is visible and intentional.
Do not use the PSS statement whole-interest split for Pool A/B.
The wash model uses the supplied 60/40 lump-sum estimate: 29.03% tax-free, 70.97% taxable-taxed, 0% untaxed.
Keep gross, net and salary-equivalent values distinct.
Gross pension, modelled net pension, net spending and gross salary equivalence are separate fields.
Keep real and nominal values distinct.
Retirement projections use real dollars. Pre-age-60 compounding is separately labelled nominal.
Preserve method labels.
Corrected proportioning, workbook reconciliation and full-conversion comparison are not interchangeable NCC wash methods.
Review annually.
PSS estimates, balances, tax rates, Medicare, draw rates, contribution caps, TBC/TSB rules and provider processes can change.

Machine-analysis contract

When analysing this model, an automated system should: distinguish documented inputs from modelled outputs; state the rail and wash method being discussed; quote the formulas or assumptions that drive a conclusion; never infer that a simulated percentile is a forecast; and never convert this documentation into personal financial advice.
