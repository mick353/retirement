PURPOSE AND BOUNDARIES
This document describes the inputs, formulas, scenario engines, governance controls and source lineage used by the Robinson Retirement Command Centre and linked V23 model. It contains no JavaScript and is intended to be readable by people, accessibility tools, search engines and AI retrieval systems.

Decision-support only. This is a personal planning model, not licensed financial, tax or legal advice. Future returns, longevity, legislation, PSS outcomes and provider implementation are uncertain. Reconfirm current rules and obtain professional advice before acting.

DETERMINISTIC MONTHLY PROJECTION
Let B be starting capital, r the annual real return, D the annual investment draw, and n = years x 12.

monthlyReturn = (1 + r)^(1/12) - 1
monthlyDraw = max(0, D) / 12
growth = (1 + monthlyReturn)^n
endingBalance = max(0, B x growth - monthlyDraw x ((growth - 1) / monthlyReturn))

If monthlyReturn is zero:
endingBalance = max(0, B - monthlyDraw x n)

The Command Centre uses D = max(0, annual spending - net PSS pension). The spending frontier evaluates annual spending from $80,000 to $140,000 in $5,000 steps, projects to age 75 at an illustrative 5% real return, and adds the selected home value for a gross-estate view.

OPERATIONAL ANNUAL LEDGER
mandatoryDraw = openingPoolA x ageBasedMinimumRate
lifestyleGap = max(0, spending - netPssPension)
actualDraw = min(openingPoolA, max(mandatoryDraw, lifestyleGap))
reinvestment = max(0, netPssPension + actualDraw - spending)
poolCDrag = openingPoolC x 0.0035
closingPoolA = max(0, openingPoolA x (1 + realReturn) - actualDraw)
closingPoolC = max(0, openingPoolC x (1 + realReturn) - poolCDrag + reinvestment)

Age-based minimum-rate assumptions in the integrated ledger:
under 65 4%; age 65-74 5%; 75-79 6%; 80-84 7%; 85-89 9%; 90-94 11%; 95+ 14%. Reconfirm current law before relying on these assumptions.

V23 DETERMINISTIC GOVERNOR
1. Apply the age-based mandatory draw from Pool A.
2. Use pension plus the required draw to meet the selected spending rule.
3. Draw extra from Pool A only if the spending gap exceeds the mandatory draw.
4. Route surplus to available super headroom when permitted, otherwise to Pool C.
5. Keep Pool B as a transit account for wash/recontribution modelling.

MONTE CARLO AND RISK
Integrated Command Centre:
- 600 reproducible seeded paths from age 60 through age 95.
- Returns sampled from a normal distribution using the selected real mean and 12% volatility.
- Sampled annual returns are clamped between -55% and +45%.
- Each year: capital = max(0, capital x (1 + sampledReturn) - annualDraw).
- Reports P10, P25, P50, P75 and P90 balance paths.

V23 stochastic lab:
- Exposes run count, expected real return, volatility, target age and guardrail levels.
- Reports estimated probabilities of clearing the selected floor, target and strong-estate levels.
- Sampling noise remains; at 500 runs V23 notes that results can move by roughly 1-3 percentage points between samples.

Sequence-risk isolation:
- Reorders the same annual returns into worst-first, smooth and best-first paths.
- Holding the arithmetic average constant isolates the effect of return order while withdrawals occur.

Stress controls:
- The one-off shock path applies the selected shock return at the chosen age and the mean return in other years.
- The V23 CPI-lag stress models a temporary real PSS-pension dip and a capital real-return drag, with investments topping up the pension shortfall.

NCC WASH AND DEATH-BENEFIT TAX
Planning component split from the supplied 60/40 estimate:
- Tax-free 29.03%.
- Taxable-taxed 70.97%.
- Untaxed 0%.
- Illustrative adult non-tax-dependant death-benefit-tax rate 17%.

Corrected proportioning method, used for V23 default analysis:
taxableRemoved = washAmount x currentTaxableShare
taxableAfter = max(0, taxableBefore - taxableRemoved)
deathBenefitTax = taxableComponent x 0.17

At a 70.97% taxable share, the first $130,000 wash removes about $92,258 taxable. The taxable share and benefit per wash decay. Illustrative corrected analysis may require up to 14 annual washes from ages 61-74 to approach a $10,000 DBT target.

Workbook separate/frozen-share method:
- Reproduces the workbook component treatment and about $15,683.86 tax reduction per wash.
- Useful for reconciliation, but freezes the share and is not the corrected proportioning default.

Full-conversion master-document comparison:
- Treats the full $130,000 as taxable converted, implying $22,100 at 17%.
- Kept as an explicit comparison and never silently mixed with proportioning.

Modelled sequence:
1. Before the relevant 30 June snapshot, confirm TSB, contribution eligibility, cap space and implementation advice.
2. Commute the selected amount from Pool A under the chosen component method.
3. Contribute the eligible NCC amount to Pool B.
4. Recommence or consolidate according to provider and advice requirements.
5. Preserve the intended $5,000 planning buffer and apply the surplus-routing governor.

The integrated Command Centre has a simplified 0-7 wash-cycle illustration. V23 contains the method comparison and expanded corrected analysis. Neither is an instruction to transact.

TAX AND SALARY EQUIVALENCE
The dashboard calculates resident individual income tax plus a 2% Medicare levy, then uses binary search to find the gross salary that produces a selected net income. The baseline function uses a $18,200 tax-free threshold; bracket points at $45,000, $135,000 and $190,000; rates of 30%, 37% and 45% above those points; and a lower bracket rate of 15% for 2026-27 or 14% for 2027-28.

The comparator excludes offsets, Medicare levy variations, Medicare levy surcharge, HELP, deductions and household-specific effects. It is not a tax-return estimate.

SPENDING AND ESTATE
V23 spending modes:
1. Age-band spending.
2. A percentage of positive investment earnings. A negative return produces no earnings-based spending gap; strong earnings can require an extra Pool A draw.
3. A percentage of the mandatory draw. This cannot exceed the mandatory draw; surplus is routed by the governor.

Simplified frontier gross estate = projected investments + selected home value.
V23 separately tracks account components, external assets and illustrative death-benefit tax.
